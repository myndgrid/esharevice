§ 02 — How it works

Three steps. No tokens, no points, no algorithmic match-making.

Trades are negotiated by the two people involved. The platform handles discovery, the ledger, and a paper-trail you can both look back on. The rest is on you and your neighbour.

Step 01

Find the thing that already exists nearby.

Search is full-text, weighted by what the listing offers, then by what its provider needs. Type "sewing machine" and you'll also surface a mending offer two streets away.

  • Distance-aware — your neighbourhood, not a national feed
  • "Wants" is searchable too: find someone after your espresso skills
  • No paid promotion — newer listings get a quiet boost

Step 02

Propose a trade with a real human.

Tell them what you have on offer and what you'd like in return. Either side can counter-propose. The thread keeps the receipts so it's clear who agreed to what.

  • One-tap counter — flip the trade, propose a different swap
  • Hold a slot for 24h while you decide together
  • If you back out twice, your listings dim until you reach out and apologise

Step 03

Settle however you settled on.

Money isn't required and never touches the platform. When both sides mark the trade settled, it goes in your ledger and both reputations tick up.

  • Free-form settlement — hours, jars of jam, "we're square"
  • Optional photo of the handoff for the trade record
  • Dispute? A neighbour you've both traded with can vouch
